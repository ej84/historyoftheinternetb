Browser Wars

In 1993, a young Marc Andreessen was always fascinated with the world wide web. Back then, however, there were only a few websites and those websites consisted of rows of boring text for the average person. Andreessen had always dreamed of everyone using the internet in their daily lives so he tried to improve it and make it user-friendly. He added picture, audio and video capabilities on the web. His best invention, however, was the world’s first graphical web browser which was called Mosaic. In 1993, they posted their innovation online and asked for beta testers in the newspaper. He got 12 replies at first but it boomed in popularity and it went from 12 - one million in months.

Bill Gates, founder of Microsoft, established a near monopoly over PC operating systems and because of that, he gained a grip over the entire computer industry. Gates dropped out of Harvard at the age of 19 to team up with his friend, Paul Allen, to make Microsoft. He wanted everyone to have desktops that would run Microsoft software and almost achieved it in 1993. About 90% of the people who owned a desktop computer were running Windows and because of this, made billions of dollars in profit. Gates, however, was very aggressive when it came down to running a business. He made many companies fall to their knees, including the popular IBM.

Andreessen teamed up with Jim Clark, founder of Silicon Graphics which made him a legend in Silicon Valley. They planned to build a new browser based on Andresseen’s past work, Mosaic.Their new company was called Netscape and when it came to the new browser they had a big sense of urgency because they were afraid that a big company, like Microsoft, would make a browser before them. Gates had doubts about the web browser and was not focused on it. However, many of his employees knew that this would change the world. Thomas Riodran, one of said employees, attempted to snoop at Netscape and was shocked when they responded with arrogance.

In 1994, Netscape’s new browser was released to the public. It was an instant success. Many companies believed that the power of the browser could be used as an alternate platform rather than being forced to use Microsoft. Gates was very worried about their new competitors and he knew that he needed to crush them.

Netscape hired an antitrust lawyer by the name of Gary Rebeck. Gary Rebeck clashed with Microsoft many times because of his clients in Silicon Valley. Netscape hired the lawyer because Microsoft had threatened them when they were having a meeting. The meeting was very controversial because Microsoft stated that everything was nice and ran smoothly in the meeting. Netscape stated otherwise and told the press that Microsoft told them to either sell their company for a million dollars or go bankrupt once they copy everything. If this was true, then Microsoft broke the antitrust law. They believed that Netscape set them up so that they may be taken down and would rise to power.

Netscape believed that they have won, however, Microsoft revealed that they were planning on making their own browser called Internet Explorer. Gates had an army of salesmen who forced people to use only their new browser and nothing else. However, the reason why Netscape lost was due to the fact that Microsoft had a lot of money. Netscape made their profit by making people pay to use their browser, however, Microsoft’s browser was entirely free and made Netscape lose the browser war.
